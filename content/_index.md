**Ferro** is a set of Rust-native rewrites of the JVM and Python data
infrastructure most teams take for granted — search, streaming,
orchestration, identity, package registries, log shipping,
monitoring. Seven product repositories, on the order of 800,000 lines
of own-code Rust, ~18,000 tests. One developer.

This site is the public face of that work. It hosts the launch
notes, ongoing engineering posts, and a directory of the
[publicly published Rust crates](/crates/) that have been extracted
from the ecosystem under Apache-2.0.

The full story of how it got here is in
[Building Ferro](/blog/building-ferro/) — the launch post.
